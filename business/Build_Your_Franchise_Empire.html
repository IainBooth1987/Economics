Franchise
A business model where a franchisor grants the right to use its brand and systems to a franchisee in exchange for fees and royalties.
Market Share
The percentage of total industry sales held by a company. Calculated as: (firm's sales ÷ total market sales) × 100.
Brand Equity
The added value a brand gives a product beyond its functional benefit. Strong brands command premium prices.
Economies of Scale
Cost advantages from increased output. As restaurants grow, fixed costs are spread over more units, reducing average costs.
Cash Flow
The net movement of money in and out of a business. Positive cash flow means more money is coming in than going out.
ROI (Return on Investment)
Measures the efficiency of an investment: (profit ÷ investment cost) × 100. Higher % = better return.
CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility)
A business's commitment to operating ethically and contributing positively to society and the environment.
Supply Chain
The network of organisations involved in producing and delivering a product from raw materials to the end consumer.
