Asset Prices

Credit conditionsTighten — LTV ratios tighten; M4 slows; riskier borrowers rationed out

Net Exports (X−M)Worsen — UK goods pricier abroad; import volumes rise (but imports cheaper reduces CPI)

ADShifts right — C↑, I↑, (X−M)↑; positive output gap may open

CPIRises toward 2% — demand-pull pressure builds; firms regain pricing power; wages rise

Key riskOverheating if rates kept too low too long (e.g. 2021–22 inflation surge)

⚖️ Key Evaluation Point — Time Lags
Both policy types face the same fundamental constraint: the full effect on CPI takes 18–24 months to materialise. This means the MPC must be forward-looking, acting on forecast inflation rather than current data. Raising rates too late risks an entrenched wage-price spiral; raising too aggressively risks an unnecessary recession. The 2022–24 UK episode illustrates both dangers — rates possibly held high too long even after inflation was clearly falling.
