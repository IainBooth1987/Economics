"Opinions are free and usually wrong. We don't argue about which letter is best — we test. Half a million late payers: the old letter and four challengers, 100,000 people each, chosen at random. The counts are coming in now — the analysis is your job."

This is a randomised controlled trial (RCT) — the gold standard of evidence. Random assignment makes the five groups alike in every other way, so any difference is caused by the letter itself. The answers

Year two: 6% × 1m = 60,000 extra on-time payers → £54m arriving on time, for £0.5m of letters — still £108 back per £1
When a nudge is not enough
  1. When the harm is large and urgent. Nobody nudges drink-driving — the cost of each failure is too high, so the law compels. The bigger the externality, the stronger the tool.
  2. When effects fade and the problem doesn't. Nudges habituate (this year's +6 proves it); taxes and mandates keep working without anyone reading a letter.
  3. When the behaviour is profitable to resist. Firms re-engineer around nudges; a sugar levy changed recipes across an entire industry because it changed the price.
  4. And the case for nudging anyway: it's nearly free, preserves choice, and politically survives where bans die — which is why the smart unit treats the ladder as a toolkit, not a contest.

How an RCT works — you just ran one

Define the outcome ROUND 3

One measurable number — here, % paying within 30 days. If you can't measure it, you can't test it.

Diagnose the behaviour ROUND 1

Which biases are actually driving it? The intervention should match the diagnosis.

Design the variants ROUND 4

Each letter changes one thing — so you know what caused any difference.

Randomise

People are assigned to letters by chance — making the groups identical in every other way. This is the magic ingredient.

Keep a control group

One group gets the old letter — the 60% baseline everything is judged against.

Run it and measure 🔬 THE TRIAL

The differences you saw were caused by the letters — nothing else could explain them.

Scale the winner — ethically ROUND 5

Effectiveness, value for money, and the publicity test, all at once.

Keep testing ROUND 6 & BEYOND

Effects fade, contexts differ, messages backfire — the trial never really ends.
