Match the idea

Match each economist on the left to their core idea on the right. Write the matching letter next to each name.

  1. Adam Smith ___
  2. David Ricardo ___
  3. Karl Marx ___
  4. Alfred Marshall ___
  5. Irving Fisher ___
  6. John Maynard Keynes ___
  7. Joseph Schumpeter ___
  8. Friedrich Hayek ___
  9. A.W. Phillips ___

  1. The invisible hand and division of labour drive markets toward efficient outcomes
  2. Comparative advantage: countries gain from trade even without an absolute advantage
  3. Capitalism's internal contradictions lead to class conflict and crisis

Round 4 — Policy Decisions

12 minutes

Warm-up: match the term

Match each policy term on the left to its correct definition on the right. Write the matching letter next to each term.

  1. Expansionary fiscal policy ___
  2. Contractionary monetary policy ___
  3. Quantitative easing ___
  4. Supply-side policy ___
  5. Automatic stabiliser ___
  6. Crowding out ___
  1. Central bank purchases of assets to inject money into the economy
  2. Raising interest rates to reduce aggregate demand and inflation
  3. Policies aimed at increasing the economy's productive capacity
  4. Increased government borrowing pushes up interest rates and reduces private investment
  5. A rise in G and/or cut in T intended to increase aggregate demand
  6. A tax or benefit that automatically reduces cyclical swings without new legislation (e.g. unemployment benefit)
