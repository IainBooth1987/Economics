How it works
Using MCQ for revision
Why multiple choice
drives better revision

Even on boards without a multiple-choice paper, MCQ practice is one of the most effective revision strategies available — for any specification.

1
Diagnosis, not just practice. A wrong answer pinpoints exactly which concept needs work — more precisely than re-reading a whole topic.
2
Distractors expose misconceptions. A-Level MCQs are built around the errors students most commonly make. Choosing a wrong answer reveals a specific misunderstanding you can then target directly.
3
Rapid coverage. Test 15–20 concepts in the time it takes to write one essay plan — ideal for broad diagnostic passes before an exam.
4
Retrieval practice works. Actively recalling knowledge strengthens memory far more than re-reading notes — even when the answer turns out to be wrong.
Adaptive learning
How the adaptive
engine supports progress

Questions aren't served at random. The engine tracks your performance across every topic and adjusts the session in real time.

1
Weighted topic selection. Topics where your score is below 40% are prioritised heavily — strong topics stay in the rotation but at low frequency.
2
Wrong-answer reinsertion. Incorrect answers are added to a reinsertion pool and resurface later in the same session — spaced repetition built into every practice run.
3
Live mastery tracker. A sidebar shows your percentage score in every topic — colour-coded from red to green — updated after every question you answer.
4
Session summary. Open a full breakdown of topic performance at any point, then use it to decide where to focus next — in this session or your next revision block.
