The Macro Vault

An A-Level Macroeconomics escape room — four rounds, one combination.

Teams work together to clear four rounds of applied macro knowledge and assemble the full vault code — every task in every round contributes a piece, so nothing can be skipped. Everything they need is in the workbook — no other equipment required.

Briefing — 2 minutes

How this works

Teams

Groups of 3–4, one workbook each. Everything is done as a team — talk through each answer together before writing it down.

Timing

50 minutes total: 10 / 15 / 8 / 12 minutes per round, plus briefing and reveal. Trim any round if you're short on time.

Code

Each round produces one part of an 8-character vault code. Get any part wrong and the vault won't open.

Hints cost 60 seconds each. First team with the full, correct code shouts "Vault!"

Round 1 — 10 minutes

Real World Economics: Rankings

As a team, rank Chancellors, Bank of England Governors, and Economists into chronological order in your workbook — then match each economist to their core idea.

Talk it through together: someone in your team probably knows a piece others don't. Vault Key 1 comes from your Governors list.

Round 2 — 15 minutes

Calculation Round

Seven questions in your workbook — multiplier, real GDP, real GDP per capita, weighted price index, GDP index numbers, the quantity theory of money, and national income equilibrium.

Work through the tables and questions as a team — check each other's working before moving on. Your vault key comes from the final part-answer of each question.

Round 4 — 12 minutes

Policy Decisions

Warm up as a team by matching each policy term to its definition, then work through three economic scenarios in your workbook. Same policy menu each time — but the right answer changes with the evidence.

Discuss the clues in each scenario together before committing to an answer — that's where the marks are.
