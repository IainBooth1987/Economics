Round 4 — Market Failure Policy Decisions

12 minutes

Warm-up: match the term

Match each term on the left to its correct definition on the right. Write the matching letter next to each term.

  1. Externality ___
  2. Merit good ___
  3. Demerit good ___
  4. Public good ___
  5. Information failure ___
  6. Moral hazard ___
  7. Tragedy of the commons ___
  8. Free rider problem ___
  1. A good that is under-consumed relative to the social optimum, often due to information failure (e.g. education, vaccination)
  2. A good that is over-consumed relative to the social optimum, often due to information failure (e.g. tobacco, sugary drinks)
  3. A cost or benefit affecting a third party not involved in a transaction
  4. Non-excludable, non-rivalrous good that the free market tends to under-provide (e.g. street lighting, national defence)
  5. Decisions are made without full or accurate information about costs, benefits, or risks
  6. Once insured or protected from risk, an individual or firm takes on greater risk than they otherwise would
  7. A shared, unregulated resource gets overused and depleted because no individual bears the full cost of their use
  8. People benefit from a good or service without paying for it, because it is non-excludable
