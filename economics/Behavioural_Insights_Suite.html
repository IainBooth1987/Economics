The EAST Framework

The Behavioural Insights Team's guide to effective nudge design

Developed by the UK's Behavioural Insights Team (BIT), EAST provides a four-part checklist for designing effective nudges. Click each principle to explore.

E

Easy

Remove friction and reduce the effort required to make the desired choice

A

Attractive

Use salience, personalisation and incentives to capture attention

S

Social

Harness the power of social norms and peer influence

T

Timely

Intervene at the right moment — ideally at a decision point

UK Nudge Unit — Real-World Policy

How the Behavioural Insights Team changed public policy

BIT Timeline

2010
Founded within 10 Downing Street (Cabinet Office) — nicknamed the "Nudge Unit", initially led by David Halpern. Inspired by Thaler & Sunstein's Nudge (2008).
2012
First major policy successes — HMRC social norm letters increased tax compliance by up to 15 percentage points. Organ donor registration wording change drove 96,000 additional sign-ups.
2014
Became a social purpose company — part-privatised, with government, Nesta and employees as shareholders. Global expansion begins.
2017
Richard Thaler wins Nobel Prize in Economics for his foundational work on nudge theory and behavioural economics.
2024
Global influence — BIT has inspired 200+ nudge units worldwide in 50+ countries. Operating in health, education, finance, energy and climate policy.

Success Stories — Bias → Policy Links

💰

HMRC Tax Compliance

Social NormsLoss Aversion

Letters to late taxpayers included messages such as "9 in 10 people in your town pay on time." The social norm framing exploited conformity bias and mild loss aversion.

↑ 15 percentage point increase in compliance
🫀

NHS Organ Donation

Default BiasFraming

Registration page changed from opt-in to a reciprocity frame: "If you needed an organ, would you take one?" Exploiting social reciprocity norms and default bias.

↑ 96,000 additional registrations (+100%)
💉

NHS Flu Vaccination

Ownership (Endowment)Timing

SMS messages framed the appointment as already reserved: "Your flu jab is booked for you — confirm now." Created endowment effect; timely nudge at booking point.

↑ Significant uptake increase among NHS staff
💼

Job Centre Action Plans

Status Quo BiasImplementation Intentions

Generic "seek work" advice replaced with specific plans: "Apply to 3 jobs on Tuesday morning." Breaking default inertia using implementation intentions.

↑ Faster re-employment with greater confidence
⚡

Household Energy Use

Social NormsAnchoring

Opower reports showed households their energy use versus neighbours. Anchoring on peer average and social norm comparison reduced consumption.

↑ 2–3% reduction in household energy use
🎓

University Applications

Availability HeuristicLoss Framing

Letters to disadvantaged students reframed university costs using loss framing and made student finance information more salient and easy to access.

↑ Increased applications from low-income groups
