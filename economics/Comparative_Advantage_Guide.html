🎯 Absolute Advantage

The ability to produce more of a good using the same resources. Country A has absolute advantage in both goods in this simulation.

⚖️ Comparative Advantage

The ability to produce a good at a lower opportunity cost. Even with absolute advantage in everything, specialisation still benefits both.

💰 Opportunity Cost

What you give up to get something else. The OC of producing cloth is the wine you could have produced instead.

📊 PPF

Shows all possible combinations of two goods producible with available resources. Points on the curve = maximum efficiency.

🤝 Terms of Trade

The exchange rate between goods. Must fall between both countries' opportunity cost ratios for mutual benefit.

🌟 Gains from Trade

Specialisation + trade = higher total world output. Both countries can consume beyond their individual PPFs.

💡 Ricardo's Revolutionary Insight

Even if one country is more efficient at everything, both benefit from trade via comparative advantage. David Ricardo, 1817.

📐 The Opportunity Cost Formula▼

Opportunity cost = what you sacrifice when making a choice.

Formula:

OC of Good X = Max Y ÷ Max X

"How many Y must I give up per unit of X?"

If max = 50 cloth OR 30 wine: per cloth = 30÷50 = 0.6 wine; per wine = 50÷30 = 1.67 cloth

📝 Worked Example▼

Technologia: Max 50 cloth OR 30 wine

OC of 1 cloth = 30 ÷ 50 = 0.6 wine

OC of 1 wine = 50 ÷ 30 = 1.67 cloth

Check: 0.6 × 1.67 ≈ 1.0 ✔ (reciprocals!)

📊 Understanding PPFs▼

ON the curve

Maximum efficiency. All resources fully used.

INSIDE the curve

Inefficiency. Unemployed/underused resources.

OUTSIDE the curve

Unattainable alone — reachable through growth or trade!

⚠️ Key distinction: Trade does not shift the PPF outward — it allows consumption beyond it. The PPF itself only shifts with economic growth (more resources or better technology).

📐 Shape, Slope & Comparison▼

Straight-line PPF = constant opportunity cost. The slope of the PPF represents the opportunity cost of the good on the x-axis (cloth).

CountryMax ClothMax WineSlopeMeaning
🏭 Technologia5030−0.6Flatter → lower OC of cloth
🍇 Rustica2024−1.2Steeper → higher OC of cloth

Key insight: The different slopes visually show the different opportunity costs. Technologia’s flatter PPF means cloth is “cheaper” to produce there (lower OC). Rustica’s steeper PPF means wine is relatively cheaper there.

📈 PPF Diagrams — Gains from Trade

Both countries specialise, then trade at the world price of 0.9 wine per cloth (within the mutually beneficial range of 0.6–1.2)

🏭 Technologia (Comparative Advantage: Cloth)

Max: 50 cloth OR 30 wine  |  OC of cloth = 0.6 wine

Technologia PPF: Straight line from (0,30) to (50,0) with slope -0.6. After specialising in cloth (50,0) and trading 15 cloth for 13.5 wine at world price 0.9, consumption moves to (35, 13.5) which is beyond the original PPF. Autarky consumption was (25, 15).

🍇 Rustica (Comparative Advantage: Wine)

Max: 20 cloth OR 24 wine  |  OC of cloth = 1.2 wine

Rustica PPF: Straight line from (0,24) to (20,0) with slope -1.2. After specialising in wine (0,24) and trading 13.5 wine for 15 cloth at world price 0.9, consumption moves to (15, 10.5) which is beyond the original PPF. Autarky consumption was (10, 12).

🌟 Gains from Trade Summary

🏭 Technologia

Autarky (50/50): 25 cloth + 15 wine

After trade: 35 cloth + 13.5 wine

✓ Gains: +10 cloth (trades −1.5 wine)

To gain 10 additional cloth domestically, Technologia would have to give up 6 wine (10 × 0.6). Through trade, it gives up only 1.5 wine.

🍇 Rustica

Autarky (50/50): 10 cloth + 12 wine

After trade: 15 cloth + 10.5 wine

✓ Gains: +5 cloth (trades −1.5 wine)

To gain 5 additional cloth domestically, Rustica would have to give up 6 wine (5 × 1.2). Through trade, it gives up only 1.5 wine.

Both countries consume at a point beyond their PPF — this is the fundamental gain from specialisation and trade.

The terms of trade line (0.9 wine/cloth) lies between both countries’ opportunity costs (0.6 and 1.2), ensuring mutual benefit.
