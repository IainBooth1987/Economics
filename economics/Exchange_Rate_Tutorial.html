What is an Exchange Rate?

An exchange rate is the price of one currency expressed in terms of another. It tells you how much of one currency is needed to obtain one unit of another.

Example: £1 = $1.27 means one British pound buys 1.27 US dollars.

Direct vs Indirect Quotes

Direct: How much home currency buys 1 unit of foreign currency.

Indirect: How much foreign currency you get for 1 home currency unit.

  • GBP, EUR, AUD, NZD → indirect (1 GBP = x USD)
  • JPY, CHF, most others → direct (1 USD = x JPY)

Conversion Rules

TO foreign currency → Multiply by rate

FROM foreign currency → Divide by rate

£500 → $ (rate 1.27): 500 × 1.27 = $635
$635 → £ (rate 1.27): 635 ÷ 1.27 = £500

Bid-Ask Spreads

Dealers quote two prices. The spread is their profit margin.

  • Bid: Rate at which the dealer buys foreign currency from you (lower)
  • Ask: Rate at which the dealer sells foreign currency to you (higher)

Cross Rates

Calculate a rate between two currencies via a third (usually USD).

Formula: EUR/GBP = (EUR/USD) ÷ (GBP/USD)

EUR/USD = 1.10, GBP/USD = 1.27
EUR/GBP = 1.10 ÷ 1.27 = 0.866
So 1 EUR = £0.866

Real-World Applications

  • International trade: UK firms paying overseas invoices
  • Tourism: Exchanging at airports & bureaux de change
  • Investment: Currency risk on foreign assets
  • Central banks: Intervention to manage competitiveness
