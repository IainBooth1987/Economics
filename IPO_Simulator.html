Quick Questions
Q1 — True or False
An IPO is when a private company first sells shares to the public.
Correct — an Initial Public Offering is the moment a company moves from private to public ownership by listing on a stock exchange.
Q2 — Multiple Choice
What does IPO stand for?
IPO stands for Initial Public Offering — the first time a company's shares are made available for public purchase.
Q3 — True or False
A higher IPO share price always means the IPO was more successful.
Not necessarily — if the price is set too high, demand falls and the share price drops after listing, which damages reputation and raises less capital than expected.
Q4 — Multiple Choice
What is the main role of an investment bank in an IPO?
Investment banks act as underwriters — they advise on valuation, help set the price, and connect the company with institutional investors.
Q5 — True or False
After an IPO, shares can be bought and sold by the public on a stock exchange.
Correct — once listed, shares trade freely on the secondary market (e.g. the London Stock Exchange), giving investors liquidity.
